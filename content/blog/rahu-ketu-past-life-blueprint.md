The nodes of the Moon, Rahu and Ketu, are not planets. They are mathematical points where the Moon's orbit intersects the ecliptic. Yet in Vedic astrology, they carry more karmic weight than any physical planet in your chart.

**Ketu shows where you've been.** The sign, house, and Nakshatra of Ketu in your birth chart reveal the qualities, skills, and tendencies you mastered in past lives. These feel natural to you, almost too natural. You default to Ketu's patterns without thinking.

**Rahu shows where you're going.** It represents the unfamiliar territory your soul chose for this lifetime. Rahu's placement feels uncomfortable, obsessive, even addictive, because you're learning something entirely new.

## The Axis as a Story

Think of Rahu-Ketu not as two separate points, but as one axis, a single line of karmic evolution. Ketu is the starting point; Rahu is the destination. Your entire life is a journey along this line.

For example, someone with Ketu in the 4th house and Rahu in the 10th house carries past-life mastery in domestic comfort, emotional security, and inner peace. Their soul's challenge this lifetime? Building something in the public sphere: career, reputation, authority. The 4th house safety feels like home; the 10th house ambition feels terrifying but necessary.

## Why This Matters for Self-Improvement

Understanding your Rahu-Ketu axis is not about predicting events. It's about understanding the fundamental tension in your life: the pull between comfort and growth, between what you know and what you need to learn.

Every major life decision, every recurring pattern, every unexplainable obsession can be traced back to this axis.
