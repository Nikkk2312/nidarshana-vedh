## How It Began

It was an ordinary evening. I came back from office at 7:30 PM. Exhausted, not just from the day, but from something deeper. I felt broken in a way I couldn't name. The kind of broken where you sit on your bed and don't know what to do next.

At 8:00 PM, for no reason I can explain, I opened something on astrology. I don't remember what led me there. Something pulled me.

I started reading. And I didn't stop.

I forgot to eat. Not because I was fasting or disciplined. I genuinely forgot. The hunger disappeared. Sleep disappeared. Time disappeared. I was so completely absorbed that the next thing I noticed was sunlight coming through the window. It was 9:00 AM. I had read through the entire night without moving.

I got up, brushed, bathed, and left for office. Running on zero sleep and zero food. But something had shifted. I felt more awake than I had in months.

That night was the beginning. Not of a hobby. Not of a career plan. Of a calling that hasn't let go of me since.

<div class="pull-quote">Of a calling that hasn't let go of me since.</div>

</div>

## The Journey Since

From that night, I started reading everything I could find. Books on Vedic astrology. Classical texts, modern interpretations, Jaimini, Parashari, Nadi systems. I didn't study astrology the way you study for an exam. I studied it the way a starving person eats. Urgently, gratefully, unable to stop.

I started reading charts for friends and family. Then for strangers. Each chart taught me something no book could. The gap between theory and a real human life sitting in front of you, asking you to make sense of their pain.

I took consultations from senior astrologers. I compared methods. I tested predictions against reality. I made mistakes and learned from them. I built my understanding bit by bit, not in a classroom, but in the quiet hours between midnight and dawn, chart after chart, year after year.

I believe there is no end to Jyotish. It is an ocean. You can study it your entire life and still find depths you haven't touched. I will keep learning until the day I die. That is not humility. It is simply the truth of how vast this science is.

<div class="pull-quote">I will keep learning until the day I die.</div>

## Why Nidarshana Vedh Exists

For a long time, I kept this knowledge to myself, reading charts for people I knew, quietly studying, not thinking about building anything public.

Then I looked around at what was happening to Vedic astrology online.

Influencers with six months of surface-level knowledge were making reels about "Rahu in 7th house" with background music and zero depth. Platforms were charging ₹150 for 10-minute consultations where software does the reading and the astrologer just narrates. Fear-mongering had become a business model. Scare people with Sade Sati, sell them a gemstone, move to the next customer.

The sanctity of Jyotish was being devoured. A science that the rishis preserved across thousands of years was being reduced to content fodder for engagement and affiliate commissions.

I realized it was a waste, no, it was wrong, to sit quietly while this happened. If I had been given the ability to understand charts, and if that understanding came not just from study but from something deeper, then keeping it to myself was not modesty. It was negligence.

<div class="pull-quote">Keeping it to myself was not modesty. It was negligence.</div>

Nidarshana Vedh exists to do one thing: **show people what real Vedic astrology looks like.** Not the Instagram version. Not the Astrotalk version. The real thing, grounded in shastras, deepened by experience, and offered with the aashirvad of the divine.

## The Mantras We Live By

<div class="mantra-card fade-in">
<div class="mantra-devanagari">ॐ काल भैरवाय नमः</div>
<div class="mantra-transliteration">Om Kaal Bhairavaay Namah</div>
<div class="mantra-explanation">
<p>Kaal Bhairav is the fierce form of Lord Shiva who governs time itself. In Jyotish, time is everything. The Dashas, the transits, the Muhurtas. Every prediction, every remedy, every reading exists within the framework of Kaal, time. We invoke Kaal Bhairav because without the blessings of the lord of time, no astrologer can truly see what the chart is revealing. He removes obstacles from the path of truth. He punishes dishonesty. He protects those who approach Jyotish with sincerity and humility.</p>
<p><em>This is not a decorative mantra on our website. It is the foundation of every reading we give.</em></p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="mantra-card fade-in">
<div class="mantra-devanagari">ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय नमः</div>
<div class="mantra-transliteration">Om Namo Bhagavate Vaasudevaay Namah</div>
<div class="mantra-explanation">
<p>This is the Dwadashaakshari Mantra, the twelve syllable mantra of Lord Vishnu as Vaasudeva. It is a mantra of surrender, of acknowledging that all knowledge, all insight, all ability to help others comes not from the self but from the divine. Lord Vishnu sustains the universe and everything in it. The planets move by His will. The karmas unfold by His design.</p>
<p>Before we open a chart, before we speak a single word of interpretation, we remember that whatever clarity comes through us is not ours. It belongs to the one who sustains all.</p>
</div>
